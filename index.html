The Exxon Valdez Oil Spill

A project by Rosie Symms

Background

On March 24 of 1989, the Exxon Valdez, an oil ship which was carrying 53 million gallons of crude oil crossed along the Prince William Sound in Alaska. The captain was drunk, and ran aground, which broke 8 cargo tanks and released around 11 million gallons of crude oil into the Prince William Sound. It was the largest oil spill in US history, and it was in a very untouched part of alaska where not much human intervention had taken place. The cleanup process took a very long time, and there is still lasting damage to the wildlife of the area.

Thesis statemnet

The Exxon Valdez oil spill was one of the biggest oil spills in history, both in magnitude and its reach. As catastrophic and destructive as it was, it has been very useful to motivate change in governmental oversight involving keeping the environment clean and not interfering, as well as being a big example of the consequences of recklessness and disregard for the environment, and having people be more aware of why environmental neglect is bad.
